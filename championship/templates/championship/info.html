Swiss Unity League

The Swiss Unity League (SUL) is a collaborative league in 2023 between various Magic tournament organizers in Switzerland with an end of year 2-day invitational tournament with 3.5k+ Prizes.

Any organizer can register their tournament for the league to make it award Qualification Points (QPs). Players need to collect these QPs in order to qualify for the invitational tournament.

SUL Invitational 18-19.11.2023

3.5k Prize Pool

In case the league has a bigger attendance than planned, the prizes will be increased further. The prizes will be awarded in the form of Cardmarket coupons.

  • 1st: € 1000
  • 2nd: € 500
  • 3rd/4th: € 350
  • 5th-8th: € 250
  • 9th-12th: € 75

How to Qualify

The season for the SUL will run from 1st of January 2023 to 10th of November 2023. During that time all tournaments participating at SUL will award Qualification Points (QPs). The top 32 players that earned the most QPs are qualified at the end of the season. If the league has a higher attendance than expected, the number of qualified players will be increased (e.g. to the top 64). In addition, players with a lot of QPs will be awarded byes for the tournament.

How to participate

Tell your local tournament organizer (TO) about our league and show them the SUL Guide for Organizers! Your TO will then register their local events for the league so you can start collecting Qualification Points at those events.

Qualification Points

Players can earn Qualification Points (QPs) at all tournaments participating in the league. QPs are awarded in the following three ways:

  • Match Points: A player receives QPs equal to the match points they earned during the swiss rounds of the tournament. (E.g. A record of 3-1-1 in swiss rounds awards 10 QPs).
  • Participation: A player receives 3 QPs just for participating at the event.
  • Tops: The Top 8/12/16 receive extra QPs as explained later.

Tournament Types

There are the following 3 types of tournaments. Your local tournament organizer will assign one of these 3 types to each tournament they organize. Depending on the type of tournament, a different amount of QPs will be awarded to the participants.

SUL Regular

All normal events (weekly, FNM, Prerelease) at your local store or association are SUL Regular events.

SUL Regional

SUL Regional events are medium-sized events for which people travel all around your canton to attend them. Each of your local tournament organizers can organize one of these events per month in each Format. So for example 1 in Modern, 1 in Limited, 1 in Pioneer and 1 in Legacy. SUL Regional events can be with or without a top 8.

SUL Premier

SUL Premier events are meant to be huge tournaments for which people travel all around Switzerland. To make those events feel special and exciting your local tournament organizer can only host up to 2 of these events per year.

Multiplier

Each of the 3 tournament types has a different multiplier that increases the number of Qualification Points the players earn. SUL Regular events have a multiplier of 1x, SUL Regional 4x, and SUL Premier 6x. The QPs earned for match points and participation (but not for tops) are multiplied with the given multiplier for the tournament.

Example: During 4 Swiss rounds Jace manages to get 7 match points with 2 wins, 1 loss, and 1 draw. Normally Jace would earn 7 QPs for the match points and 3 QPs for participation, which is 10 QPs in total. However since the event has a multiplier of 4x, he receives 40 QPs instead.

Each player can earn a maximum of 500 QPs with SUL Regular events in total, further points will be ignored. With SUL Regional and Premier events a player can earn an unlimited amount of points.

Qualification Points for Top 8

For SUL Regional events running a top 8 is optional, so the tournament organizer can decide if they wish to run a top 8 or not. For SUL Premier events running a top 8 is mandatory. Events with top 8 award additional Qualification Points according to the schema below.

The QPs for 9th-12th and 13th-16th are only awarded if the tournament has more than 32 or 48 participants.

Example

Chandra plays a SUL Premier event at her local gaming store (hence it has a multiplier of 6x). During the 5 Swiss rounds she manages to get 3 wins, 2 losses, and 1 draw (10 match points). She places 11th in the overall ranking.

Therefore she receives 60 QPs (6x10) for the match points, 18 QPs (6x3) just for participating. Since the event has more than 32 players, she receives an additional 75 QPs for placing 11th. With this event she earns a grand total of 153 QPs.

Gideon plays a SUL Regional event without top 8 at his local gaming store (hence it has a multiplier of 4x). During the 5 Swiss rounds he manages to get 3 wins and 1 loss (9 match points). Therefore he receives 36 QPs (4x9) for the match points, 12 QPs (4x3) just for participating. Since the event has no top 8, no additional QPs are awarded. With this event he earns a grand total of 48 QPs.

As mentioned before, the QPs from all tournaments are summed up and the 32 players with the most QPs at the end of the season (10.11.2023) will be qualified for the invitational.

Byes

The best performing players at the league are awarded byes (free wins in the first rounds) for the invitational tournament at the end of the year. A player can have at maximum 2 byes, further byes will be ignored. Players can earn byes in the following ways:

  • The top 5 players in the end of year QP-leaderboard will be awarded > 1 bye.
  • The player of the year (with the most QPs) will be awarded 2 byes.
  • SUL Premier events with more than 128 participants award 2 byes to > the winner of the tournament.

Team Events

Team events where teams of 3 players are playing 1v1 matches against a player of a different team can also be part of SUL. In this case each player receives QPs equal to the match points their team earned in the swiss rounds and 3 participations QPs (all multiplied with the given multiplier). The QPs awarded for top 8 will be divided equally among the team members and rounded up. (E.g. if the winning team earns 500 QPs from top 8, then each team member gets 167 QPs)

How do we finance this ?

In addition to sponsorships, tournament organizers will pay a small fee for each participant of the Regional and Premier events they organize. For detailed information, see the SUL Guide for Organizers!
